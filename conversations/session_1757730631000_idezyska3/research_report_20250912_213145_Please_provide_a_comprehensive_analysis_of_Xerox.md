**Concept Definition:**
Multi-Agent Orchestration is a sophisticated AI framework where a central system coordinates the actions of multiple, specialized AI "agents" to complete a complex, multi-step business process. Instead of relying on a single, monolithic AI model, this system acts as a "general contractor," assigning different parts of a task to the best-suited agent—whether it's a large language model (LLM) for text generation, a computer vision model for image analysis, or a robotic process automation (RPA) bot for data entry.

**Strategic Goal:**
The goal is to create autonomous business processes that can handle complex workflows from end-to-end with minimal human intervention. For example, an orchestrated system could handle an entire insurance claim process:
1.  An **Ingestion Agent** scans and digitizes the claim form.
2.  A **Data Extraction Agent** uses AI to pull key information (policy number, incident details).
3.  A **Validation Agent** cross-references the information with the policyholder's database.
4.  A **Generative AI Agent** drafts a response letter to the customer.
5.  An **RPA Agent** initiates the payment process in the financial system.

By orchestrating these agents, Xerox aims to offer a powerful platform for hyper-automation, positioning itself as a key enabler of the AI-driven enterprise.
